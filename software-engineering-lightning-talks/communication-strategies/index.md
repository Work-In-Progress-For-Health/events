# Communication strategies

Here are communication strategies that can improve your teamwork, your presentations, and your ideas for improvements.

## Understanding the Barriers

1. **Timing** - Even great ideas can fail if introduced at the wrong time

2. **Context** - People might lack the background to appreciate the solution

3. **Competing Priorities** - Other urgent matters drowning out your proposal

4. **Risk Aversion** - Fear of change, even with proven solutions

5. **Communication Style** - Technical excellence ≠ persuasive presentation

## Effective Communication Strategies

### Start with the Problem, not the Solution

Instead of: "We should implement X."

Try: "We're spending 20% of our time on this area: (then describe it briefly). Here's how we could save time that by trying X."


### Use the "What's In It For Them" (WIIFT) Approach

Map your idea to what each stakeholder cares about:

- **Managers**: Cost savings, risk reduction, timeline improvements

- **Developers**: Less tedious work, better tools, career growth

- **Operations**: Fewer emergencies, easier maintenance

- **Business**: Faster features, competitive advantage

### Create a Proof of Concept

Small Win Strategy:

1. Implement a tiny version that demonstrates value

2. Measure concrete improvements

3. Share results, not just theory

4. Let success build momentum

### Build Allies First

- Start with one supportive team member

- Get their feedback and buy-in

- Have them help present to others

- Create a coalition before going to the full team

### Document Everything

Create a simple one-pager with:

- **Problem Statement** (2-3 sentences)

- **Proposed Solution** (bullet points)

- **Evidence/Proof** (metrics, case studies)

- **Implementation Plan** (phases, timeline)

- **Risks & Mitigation**

## Communication Techniques

### The STAR Method

- **Situation**: Current state and pain points

- **Task**: What needs to change

- **Action**: Your proven solution

- **Result**: Expected outcomes with evidence

### The Three-Part Story

1. **"Here's where we are"** (current problems)

2. **"Here's where we could be"** (vision)

3. **"Here's how we get there"** (your idea)

### Use Different Channels

- **Written proposal** for thinkers who need time

- **Quick demo** for visual learners

- **Informal coffee chat** for relationship builders

- **Data presentation** for analytical minds

## Overcoming Specific Objections

### "We don't have time"

→ "This will save X hours per week after Y weeks of investment"

### "It's too risky"

→ "Here's how we can test it safely with rollback plans"

### "We've always done it this way"

→ "True, and it served us well. Here's what's changed..."

### "It's too complex"

→ "Let me show you a simple first step"

## Example Email Template

```
Subject: Reducing Our Deployment Time by 60% - Quick Proposal

Hi Team,

**Problem**: We currently spend ~8 hours per week on deployments (based on last month's data).

**Proven Solution**: I've successfully implemented [solution] at [previous context], which reduced deployment time from 8 hours to 3 hours weekly.

**Proof**: [Link to metrics/case study]

**Next Step**: Could we discuss a small pilot in Thursday's meeting? I've prepared a 5-minute demo.

**No commitment needed** - just looking for feedback on a low-risk test.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

## Red Flags to Avoid

❌ **Don't**:

- Criticize current approaches too harshly

- Present as "my way or highway"

- Overwhelm with technical details

- Assume everyone sees what you see

- Get defensive when questioned

✅ **Do**:

- Acknowledge current system's strengths

- Invite collaboration and feedback

- Focus on business value

- Use analogies and simple examples

- Stay curious about objections

## Building Long-term Influence

### Track Record Strategy

- Start with tiny wins

- Document all successes

- Build credibility over time

- Become the go-to person for innovations

### Teaching Approach

- Share knowledge freely

- Write internal blog posts

- Give lunch-and-learn talks

- Make others look good

### Political Capital

- Support others' ideas first

- Give credit generously

- Build relationships before you need them

- Understand decision-making dynamics

## Questions for Self-Reflection

1. Is this the right problem to solve now?

2. Who would be the biggest champion if convinced?

3. What's the smallest version that would show value?

4. What competing priorities exist?

5. How can I make this a win for everyone?

## Action Plan Template

Week 1: Document current pain points with data

Week 2: Build minimal proof of concept

Week 3: Get feedback from one ally

Week 4: Refine and demo to small group

Week 5: Present to broader team with ally support

Week 6: Implement pilot with success metrics

## Remember

Even the best ideas need the right packaging, timing, and approach. Your technical solution might be perfect, but the human side of change management is equally important.
