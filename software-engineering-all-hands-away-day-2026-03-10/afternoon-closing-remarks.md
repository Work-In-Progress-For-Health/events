# Afternoon Closing Remarks

20 minutes. After sessions 5, 6, 7, 8. Finishes the entire day event.

## Slide 1: What We Built This Afternoon

Four sessions. Four steps from ambition to action.

5. **Moonshot Mission** — We named a massive problem and imagined leveraging science, technology, and innovation to solve it. We confronted fears of danger to patients, resistance to innovation, and scarcity of resources. We answered with covision, coaction, and coalescing. We see it. We build it. We live it.

6. **Lunar Lander** — We identified small independent pieces we can start right now. Double-diamond. Three amigos for discovery and design. Pizza-team for development and delivery. We asked ourselves: are we aligned enough, agile enough, cloud enough? We reached for shared hymnbooks, agile principles, and cloud sandboxes.

7. **Ground Control** — We faced measurement honestly. One size doesn't fit all. We need demo areas. We need baseline open-source success, rainbow deployments, and product-service ITIL 5.

8. **Where do we go from here?** — Software engineering is a profession. BOK and COP pairing. CPD for FY2026. We named overwhelm, discomfort, and same-as-it-ever-was as fears — and we returned to our original levers: involvement, empowerment, encouragement.

## Slide 2: The Day in Three Words

This morning we opened with three words. This afternoon we close with the same three.

- **Involvement** — Today everyone was in the room. Everyone spoke. Everyone contributed. That must continue.
- **Empowerment** — Today we named our Dreic Hylaw, our skillful dragons. Empowered professionals who lead with skill, not permission.
- **Encouragement** — Today we admitted fears honestly and named levers hopefully. That balance of honesty and hope is encouragement.

These three words are not a slogan. They are a practice. They only work if we do them every week, not once a year.

The day does not end when we leave this room. It ends when we act on what we said.

If something mattered to you today, own it. Put your name next to it. That is what empowerment looks like.
