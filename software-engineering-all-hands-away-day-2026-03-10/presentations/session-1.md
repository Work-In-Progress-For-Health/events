# Session 1: Where Are We Going?

_15-minute talk + 15-minute workshop_

---

## Slides

### Slide 1: Can you imagine the future in exactly four years?

Four years from now it will be 2030.

Think about what NHS Wales could look like. Think about what your work could look like. Think about what our patients and communities could experience differently.

Four years is long enough to transform how we build software, how we deliver services, and how we support the people of Wales. It is also short enough that we must start today.

### Slide 2: Can you imagine north stars for clinicians and patients?

Every line of code we write, every system we design, every test we run -- it ultimately serves a clinician caring for a patient, or a patient navigating their own health.

What if clinicians had tools that anticipated their needs? What if patients felt genuinely empowered to understand and manage their own wellbeing? What if our software reduced friction instead of adding to it?

These are our north stars.

### Slide 3: Can you imagine entire population dynamics?

We serve 3 million people across Wales. Our work touches population health at scale -- screening programmes, vaccination rollouts, chronic disease management, emergency response.

What if we could see the health of the nation in real time? What if we could model interventions before we deploy them? What if the software we build could help shift outcomes for entire communities?

That is the scale of our opportunity.

### Slide 4: Top Fears

- **Cardiac collapse** -- the fear of sudden, catastrophic health events, and the urgency of building systems that enable rapid response
- **Cancer** -- the fear of a disease that touches every family, and the challenge of building pathways that catch it earlier and treat it better
- **Dementia** -- the fear of losing cognitive capacity at population scale, and the challenge of building systems that can support long-term, complex care journeys

### Slide 5: Top Levers

- **Involvement** -- bringing everyone into the conversation, from frontline clinicians to patients to every member of this team
- **Empowerment** -- giving people the tools, the authority, and the confidence to act
- **Encouragement** -- creating a culture where people support each other, celebrate progress, and sustain momentum through difficulty

## Advice for event creators

### Brainstorms and ideas

- Open with a personal story or a patient story (anonymised) that illustrates why the future matters. Make the abstract concrete.
- Use the "four years from now" framing to invite people to dream without the constraints of current politics, budgets, or org charts.
- Reference the NHS Wales 10-year plan and how software engineering is a core enabler, not a back-office function.
- Consider showing a brief timeline of what NHS Wales technology looked like four years ago (2022) to illustrate how much can change.

### Insights

- People in technical roles often feel disconnected from patient outcomes. This session is an opportunity to rebuild that connection.
- The three fears (dementia, cardiac collapse, cancer) are not just clinical categories -- they are deeply personal fears that everyone in the room shares. Use that shared humanity to create common purpose.
- The three levers (involvement, empowerment, encouragement) will bookend the day in Session 8. Plant these seeds deliberately so the callback resonates.

## Workshop ideas

### Activity: Four Years From Now (15 minutes)

**Format:** Small groups of 4-5 people, mixed roles (engineers, designers, analysts, testers, security)

**Step 1 (5 minutes):** Each person writes on a sticky note (or digital equivalent) one thing they would love to be true about NHS Wales software in 2030. No constraints, no caveats -- just aspiration.

**Step 2 (5 minutes):** Groups share their sticky notes and cluster them into themes. Common themes might include: speed, simplicity, patient empowerment, data-driven decisions, interoperability, or joy in work.

**Step 3 (5 minutes):** Each group selects their single most inspiring theme and writes a one-sentence "north star statement" for it. Groups share these aloud with the room.

**Facilitator tip:** Capture these north star statements visibly on a wall or shared board. They will be referenced throughout the day as the sessions build on this opening vision.

**Alternative activity:** If the group is large, use a live polling tool (e.g. Mentimeter or Slido) to ask "What is the single biggest improvement we could make for patients through better software?" and display the word cloud in real time. Use the results to spark a brief open discussion.
