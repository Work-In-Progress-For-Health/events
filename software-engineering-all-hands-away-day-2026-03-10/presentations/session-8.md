# Session 8: Where Do We Go From Here?

_15-minute talk + 15-minute workshop_

---

## Slides

### Slide 1: Software engineering is a profession and we must step up to this

Software engineering is not just a job. It is a profession.

Doctors have a profession. Nurses have a profession. Pharmacists have a profession. Each comes with a body of knowledge, a code of ethics, a commitment to continuous learning, and a responsibility to the people they serve.

Software engineers in NHS Wales serve the same patients. Our code runs in clinical systems. Our databases hold sensitive records. Our interfaces guide clinical decisions. The quality of our work directly affects the quality of care.

We must step up to this. Not with arrogance, but with the quiet confidence of professionals who know their craft matters and who commit to practising it with excellence.

### Slide 2: SWE Body Of Knowledge (BOK) & SWE Community Of Practice (COP) pairing

Every profession has a body of knowledge -- the shared understanding of what practitioners need to know and be able to do.

The Software Engineering Body of Knowledge (SWEBOK) gives us that foundation. It covers requirements, design, construction, testing, maintenance, configuration management, engineering management, quality, and more.

But knowledge alone is not enough. Knowledge must be practised, shared, and grown in community. That is where our Community of Practice (COP) comes in.

Pairing BOK with COP means: we study together, we practise together, we grow together. The body of knowledge tells us what to learn. The community of practice gives us the space to learn it.

### Slide 3: Continuous Professional Development (CPD) and what you want for training in FY2026

What do you want to learn this year?

Continuous Professional Development is not a box to tick. It is an investment in yourself and in the people you serve. Every new skill you gain, every technique you master, every perspective you adopt makes our software better and our patients safer.

FY2026 is your year. What training will make the biggest difference for you and for NHS Wales? Cloud architecture? Test automation? Accessibility? Security? Leadership? AI and machine learning? User research?

Tell us. We are listening. And we are committed to helping you grow, because your growth is our collective strength.

### Slide 4: Top Fears

- **Overwhelm** -- the fear that there is too much to learn, too much to change, too much being asked of us on top of our daily work
- **Discomfort** -- the fear that professional development means exposing gaps in our knowledge, admitting what we do not know, and feeling like beginners again
- **Same as it ever was** -- the fear that despite all the ambition of today, nothing will actually change, and we will return to business as usual on Monday morning

### Slide 5: Top Levers (redux of Session 1)

- **Involvement** -- you are part of this. Your voice matters. Your ideas matter. Your participation is not optional for our success; it is essential.
- **Empowerment** -- you have permission to grow. You have permission to lead. You have permission to try new things and to fail and to learn.
- **Encouragement** -- we will support each other. We will celebrate each other's growth. We will not let each other face the hard parts alone.

## Advice for event creators

### Brainstorms and ideas

- This is the closing session. The tone should be warm, grounded, and forward-looking. Not a pep talk, but a genuine commitment.
- The callback to Session 1's levers (involvement, empowerment, encouragement) is the emotional bookend of the day. Make it explicit: "We started this morning with three words. We end with the same three words. Because they are not just words -- they are promises."
- Make the CPD discussion practical and specific. Bring a list of training opportunities, courses, certifications, and resources that are actually available. If budgets are limited, include free and open-source learning paths.
- Consider having a senior leader make a brief, authentic commitment: "I will personally ensure that every person in this room gets at least X days of professional development this year."
- Address the "same as it ever was" fear head-on. Name specific commitments that will change as a result of today. Even small ones: "By next month, we will have published our first shared OKRs." "By next quarter, we will have launched our first cloud sandbox." Concrete beats aspirational.
- The SWEBOK reference is important for professionalisation. Many software engineers in the NHS may not know it exists. Introduce it as a resource, not a requirement.

### Insights

- Returning to involvement, empowerment, and encouragement creates a powerful structural and emotional arc. These three words should feel different at the end of the day than they did at the beginning -- richer, more specific, more personal.
- Overwhelm is the most common fear in any change initiative. Acknowledge it without dismissing it: "Yes, there is a lot. You do not have to do it all. You do not have to do it alone. Pick one thing. Start there."
- "Same as it ever was" is the most dangerous fear because it can become a self-fulfilling prophecy. Combat it with visible, committed follow-up actions that begin before the next away day.
- Discomfort is a sign of growth. Reframe it: "If you feel uncomfortable, it means you are learning. That is exactly where you need to be."

## Workshop ideas

### Activity: My Next Step (15 minutes)

**Format:** Individual reflection first, then pairs, then whole room

**Step 1 (4 minutes):** Each person writes on a card or sticky note, privately:

- **One thing I learned today** that I want to carry forward
- **One thing I will do differently** starting next week
- **One thing I want to learn** in FY2026

**Step 2 (4 minutes):** Pair up with someone you do not usually work with. Share your three answers. Listen to theirs. Find one thing you have in common.

**Step 3 (4 minutes):** Pairs join into groups of four. Each group identifies the most common "thing I want to learn" and writes it on a large sticky note. Post them on the wall.

**Step 4 (3 minutes):** Facilitator reads the learning requests aloud. This becomes a live training needs analysis for FY2026. Commit to publishing the results and acting on them. Close with: "Involvement. Empowerment. Encouragement. Thank you."

**Facilitator tip:** The closing moment matters. End on time. End with warmth. End with a clear next step that people can hold onto. If possible, send a follow-up email within 48 hours summarising the commitments made today and the training requests gathered. This is how you combat "same as it ever was."

**Alternative activity:** "Letter to future me." Each person writes a short letter to themselves, to be opened in six months. What do they hope will have changed? What do they commit to doing? Collect the letters in sealed envelopes. The event organisers mail them out in six months as a reminder and a prompt for reflection.
