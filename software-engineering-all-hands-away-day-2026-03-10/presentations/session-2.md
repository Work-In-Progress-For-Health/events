# Session 2: Where Are Our Maps and Missions?

_15-minute talk + 15-minute workshop_

---

## Slides

### Slide 1: How do you aim for goals and guides?

Session 1 showed us where we want to go. Now we need maps to get there.

A goal without a plan is just a wish. A map without a destination is just wandering. We need both -- the clarity of direction and the structure of a plan.

In software engineering, our maps are our strategies and our missions are the concrete objectives that move us forward. Goals tell us what success looks like. Guides tell us which way to turn when the path forks.

### Slide 2: How do you say yes or no or why or when?

Every day we face decisions. Should we build this feature? Should we fix this bug first? Should we invest in this technology?

The power of strategy is not just knowing what to do -- it is knowing what NOT to do. Saying no with clarity and kindness is one of the most valuable skills a team can develop.

"Why" gives us purpose. "When" gives us sequencing. Together they turn ambition into action.

### Slide 3: How do you create protected time for help?

Strategy fails without space. If every hour of every day is consumed by urgent work, there is no room for important work.

Protected time is time set aside for planning, for learning, for helping each other. It is not a luxury -- it is a necessity. The teams that protect their time to think are the teams that build the best software.

How do we protect time to help each other grow?

### Slide 4: Top Fears

- **The cart** -- putting the cart before the horse, rushing to build before understanding the problem, delivering solutions that miss the mark because we skipped discovery
- **The whip** -- crack-the-whip, whiplash from constant change, too much failure mode where teams are stretched thin and burning out from relentless urgency
- **The fog** -- the fog of war, flying in fog without orientation, where teams cannot see the bigger picture and operate in isolation without context or direction

### Slide 5: Top Levers

- **Target Operating Model (TOM)** -- the blueprint for how we organise, operate, and deliver as a software engineering function across NHS Wales
- **Objectives & Key Results (OKRs)** -- the framework for setting ambitious goals and measuring meaningful outcomes at every level
- **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)** -- the metrics that tell us whether we are actually making progress or just staying busy

## Advice for event creators

### Brainstorms and ideas

- Use a visual metaphor throughout this session: a literal map. Show a hand-drawn map with landmarks, hazards, and destinations. It does not need to be polished -- the informality reinforces that strategy is a living, evolving thing.
- Contrast "busy" with "productive." Many teams confuse activity with progress. This session should challenge that assumption gently.
- Share an example of a real project where saying "no" or "not yet" led to a better outcome.
- Explain TOM, OKRs, and KPIs without jargon. Many staff will have heard these terms but may not feel confident using them. Demystify.

### Insights

- The three fears map to common failure modes in large organisations: premature execution (cart), unsustainable pace (whip), and lack of visibility (fog). Naming them reduces their power.
- OKRs work best when they are co-created, not imposed. If this is new to the audience, emphasise that OKRs are a dialogue, not a directive.
- Protected time is culturally difficult in the NHS, where urgency is genuine and constant. Acknowledge this tension honestly rather than pretending it does not exist.

## Workshop ideas

### Activity: Map Your Mission (15 minutes)

**Format:** Small groups of 4-5 people, ideally the same groups as Session 1

**Step 1 (3 minutes):** Revisit the north star statement from Session 1. Now ask: "What are the three biggest obstacles between us and that north star?" Each person writes one obstacle per sticky note.

**Step 2 (4 minutes):** Groups cluster the obstacles and identify their top two. For each obstacle, write one OKR-style objective: "We will [achieve X] as measured by [Y]."

**Step 3 (4 minutes):** Groups discuss: "What would we need to say NO to in order to focus on these objectives?" Write down at least one thing the group would deprioritise.

**Step 4 (4 minutes):** Each group shares their OKR and their "no" with the room. Facilitator captures these on the shared board alongside the Session 1 north stars.

**Facilitator tip:** The "no" exercise is often the most powerful part. People rarely get permission to deprioritise. Give them that permission explicitly and watch the energy shift.

**Alternative activity:** Run a "protected time audit" where each person privately estimates what percentage of their week is spent on urgent-but-not-important work vs. important-but-not-urgent work. Share anonymised averages and discuss what one change could shift the balance by 10%.
